Honda Soichiro

The man who walked against the wind


1906: Born in Iwata District, Shizuoka

Early years: Created a stamp to forge his family seal out of a used rubber bicycle pedal cover

1922: Without any formal education, left home and headed to Tokyo to look for work at a garage

1923: After Great Kantō earthquake, when most mechanics were busy rebuilding their homes, got a chance to do the job of his dreams

1928: Received the first of his 150 patents for the idea of replacing wooden spokes of a car wheel with metal. Started his own auto repair business at the age of 22

1936: Raced a turbocharged Ford in the "1st Japan Automobile Race" at Tamagawa Speedway

1937: Founded Tōkai Seiki to produce piston rings for Toyota

1946: Sold the salvageable remains of the company to Toyota to found the Honda Technical Research Institute

1948: Began to manufacture motorcycles and engines as president of Honda Motor Corporation, which has become the world's leading motorcycle manufacturer

1960s: Contrary to the headwind from the Ministry of Industry, Honda began producing cars

1970s: During the oil crisis, contrary to the decision of the cartel of automakers increased the production of low-cost cars, which conquered the market

Last years: Founded two non-profit organizations: Honda Foundation and International Association of Traffic and Safety Sciences. Enjoyed skiing, golf, racing cars, hang gliding and ballooning even at 77

All life He considered practice more important than theory, loved working robe more than suit, despised the bureaucracy and judged people by their actions
